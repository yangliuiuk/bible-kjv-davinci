1 Now it came to pass, as David sat in his house, that David said to Nathan the prophet, Lo, I dwell in an house of cedars, but the ark of the covenant of the LORD remaineth under curtains.

This verse is describing a conversation between King David and the prophet Nathan. David is expressing his gratitude to God for allowing him to live in a house made of cedar, a luxurious material. However, he is also expressing his humility by noting that the Ark of the Covenant, which is a symbol of God's presence, remains under curtains, a much simpler material. This verse is emphasizing the importance of humility and gratitude in the eyes of God.

2 Then Nathan said unto David, Do all that is in thine heart; for God is with thee.

This verse is a reminder to David that God is with him and will support him in whatever he sets out to do. Nathan is encouraging David to follow his heart and trust that God will be with him in his endeavors. This verse is a reminder that God is always with us and will help us in our endeavors if we trust in Him.

3 And it came to pass the same night, that the word of God came to Nathan, saying,

In 1 Chronicles 17:3, the verse is describing an event that happened in the same night. It states that the word of God came to Nathan, a prophet of Israel, and spoke to him. This could mean that God revealed something to Nathan in a dream or vision, or that He spoke to him directly. Whatever the case, it was a divine revelation from God to Nathan.

4 Go and tell David my servant, Thus saith the LORD, Thou shalt not build me an house to dwell in:

This verse is from the Bible and is part of a message from God to King David. God is telling David that he will not be allowed to build a house for God to live in. Instead, God will build a house for David and his descendants. This is a reminder that God does not need a physical house to live in, and that He is always with His people.

5 For I have not dwelt in an house since the day that I brought up Israel unto this day; but have gone from tent to tent, and from one tabernacle to another.

This verse is referring to the time when God was leading the Israelites through the wilderness. God had not lived in a house since the time he brought the Israelites out of Egypt, but instead had been living in tents and tabernacles as they traveled. This verse is emphasizing God's faithfulness to the Israelites and his commitment to them, even when they were living in temporary dwellings.

6 Wheresoever I have walked with all Israel, spake I a word to any of the judges of Israel, whom I commanded to feed my people, saying, Why have ye not built me an house of cedars?

This verse is referring to God speaking to the judges of Israel, who were responsible for leading and caring for the people of Israel. God is asking why they have not built Him a house of cedars, which was a symbol of His presence among the people. God is reminding the judges of their responsibility to provide for His people and to build a house of cedars to honor Him.

7 Now therefore thus shalt thou say unto my servant David, Thus saith the LORD of hosts, I took thee from the sheepcote, even from following the sheep, that thou shouldest be ruler over my people Israel:

This verse is from the Bible and is part of a prophecy given to King David by the Lord. In it, the Lord is telling David that He chose him to be the ruler of His people Israel. He reminds David that he was taken from the sheepcote, where he was tending to his father's sheep, and that the Lord has chosen him to be the leader of His people. The Lord is telling David that He has chosen him to be the leader of His people and that He will be with him and guide him in his rule.

8 And I have been with thee whithersoever thou hast walked, and have cut off all thine enemies from before thee, and have made thee a name like the name of the great men that are in the earth.

This verse is referring to God's promise to King David. God is telling David that He has been with him wherever he has gone and has protected him from his enemies. He also promises to make David's name great, like the names of the great men of the earth. This is a reminder of God's faithfulness and protection to His people.

9 Also I will ordain a place for my people Israel, and will plant them, and they shall dwell in their place, and shall be moved no more; neither shall the children of wickedness waste them any more, as at the beginning,

This verse is referring to God's promise to provide a safe and secure home for His people, Israel. He will plant them in a place and they will remain there, safe from the wickedness of their enemies. God will protect them from any further harm, just as He did at the beginning when He first brought them into the Promised Land.

10 And since the time that I commanded judges to be over my people Israel. Moreover I will subdue all thine enemies. Furthermore I tell thee that the LORD will build thee an house.

This verse is part of a promise from God to King David. God is telling David that He will continue to protect and guide the people of Israel, and that He will subdue all of David's enemies. Additionally, God promises to build David a house, which is a symbol of God's favor and blessing. This promise is fulfilled in the reign of David's son, Solomon, who builds the Temple in Jerusalem.

11 And it shall come to pass, when thy days be expired that thou must go to be with thy fathers, that I will raise up thy seed after thee, which shall be of thy sons; and I will establish his kingdom.

This verse is referring to God's promise to King David that his descendants would continue to rule after him. God promises that when David's days are over, he will raise up one of David's sons to take his place and establish his kingdom. This is a reminder that God's promises are sure and that He will always keep His word.

This verse is referring to God's promise to King David. God promises to build a house for David, which is a reference to the Temple of Jerusalem. God also promises to establish David's throne forever, meaning that his descendants will continue to rule over Israel. This is a sign of God's favor and blessing on David and his descendants.

13 I will be his father, and he shall be my son: and I will not take my mercy away from him, as I took it from him that was before thee:

This verse is referring to God's promise to King David that he will be a father to him and that his mercy will never be taken away. God is saying that He will not take away His mercy from David, as He did with the previous king. This is a reminder that God is faithful and will always keep His promises. He will never take away His mercy from those who trust in Him.

14 But I will settle him in mine house and in my kingdom for ever: and his throne shall be established for evermore.

This verse is referring to God's promise to King David. God is promising to make David's kingdom and throne last forever. He will settle David in His house and His kingdom, and His throne will be established forever. This is a sign of God's faithfulness and His commitment to His people. It is a reminder that God is faithful and will keep His promises.

This verse is referring to the words and vision that the prophet Nathan had spoken to King David. It is saying that Nathan spoke to David in accordance with the words and vision that he had seen. This is emphasizing the accuracy and truthfulness of Nathan's words to David.

16 And David the king came and sat before the LORD, and said, Who am I, O LORD God, and what is mine house, that thou hast brought me hitherto?

In this verse, King David is expressing his humility and gratitude to God for all the blessings He has bestowed upon him. He acknowledges that he is nothing compared to God and that his house is nothing compared to God's greatness. He is asking God to continue to bless him and his house, despite his unworthiness.

17 And yet this was a small thing in thine eyes, O God; for thou hast also spoken of thy servant’s house for a great while to come, and hast regarded me according to the estate of a man of high degree, O LORD God.

This verse is a prayer of thanks from King David to God. He is expressing his gratitude for the way God has blessed him and his family. He acknowledges that the blessings he has received are small in comparison to what God has promised for the future. He recognizes that God has looked upon him with favor and has given him a high status. He is humbled by God's generosity and grace.

18 What can David speak more to thee for the honour of thy servant? for thou knowest thy servant.

This verse is from a passage in which God is speaking to King David and telling him that He will make his kingdom great and that his descendants will be kings. The verse is asking David what more can he say to God to honor his servant (David). God is acknowledging that He knows David and his faithfulness, so there is nothing more that David can say to honor himself.

19 O LORD, for thy servant’s sake, and according to thine own heart, hast thou done all this greatness, in making known all these great things.

This verse is expressing gratitude to God for His greatness and power. It acknowledges that God has done great things for His servant, and that He has done them out of His own heart, not out of any obligation or expectation. The verse is a reminder that God is always faithful and loving, and that He will always provide for His people.

This verse is expressing the greatness of God and His uniqueness. It is acknowledging that there is no one like God and that He is the only true God. It is a declaration of faith and trust in God, and a recognition of His power and authority.

This verse is referring to the special relationship between God and the nation of Israel. God chose Israel to be His own people and to make them a great and powerful nation. This verse is referring to God's covenant with the Israelites. God chose the Israelites to be His own people and promised to be their God forever. He promised to protect them and guide them, and to be with them always. This verse is a reminder of God's faithfulness and His commitment to His people.

This verse is a prayer from King David to God, asking that the promises God has made to him and his house be fulfilled and remain in effect forever. David is asking God to keep His word and do as He has said. He is expressing his faith in God and His promises, and asking for God's help in ensuring that they come to pass.

This verse is referring to God's promise to King David that he would build a house for him. David was so grateful for this promise that he found it in his heart to pray to God in thanksgiving. This verse is referring to God's promise to David that his kingdom would be established and endure forever. The verse is expressing David's faith in God's promise and his recognition that God is the one who has made this promise and is able to fulfill it.

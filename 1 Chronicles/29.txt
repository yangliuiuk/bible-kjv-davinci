1 Furthermore David the king said unto all the congregation, Solomon my son, whom alone God hath chosen, is yet young and tender, and the work is great: for the palace is not for man, but for the LORD God.

In this verse, King David is addressing the congregation and reminding them that his son, Solomon, whom God has chosen, is still young and inexperienced. He is emphasizing that the task of building the palace for the Lord is a great one, and that it is not a task for man, but for the Lord God. He is emphasizing the importance of the task and the need for God's help in completing it.

2 Now I have prepared with all my might for the house of my God the gold for things to be made of gold, and the silver for things of silver, and the brass for things of brass, the iron for things of iron, and wood for things of wood; onyx stones, and stones to be set, glistering stones, and of divers colours, and all manner of precious stones, and marble stones in abundance.

This verse is describing the materials that King David has gathered to build the temple of God. He has gathered gold, silver, brass, iron, wood, onyx stones, glistering stones of various colors, precious stones, and marble stones in abundance. This shows the great effort and dedication that King David put into building the temple of God. He wanted to make sure that the temple was built with the best materials available.

3 Moreover, because I have set my affection to the house of my God, I have of mine own proper good, of gold and silver, which I have given to the house of my God, over and above all that I have prepared for the holy house,

This verse is referring to King David's generous donation to the house of God. He has given of his own wealth, both gold and silver, to the house of God, in addition to all the other preparations he has made for the holy house. This shows his great love and devotion to God and his willingness to give generously to the house of God.

4 Even three thousand talents of gold, of the gold of Ophir, and seven thousand talents of refined silver, to overlay the walls of the houses withal:

This verse is referring to the amount of gold and silver that King David and the people of Israel had collected to build the temple of the Lord. The three thousand talents of gold was from the gold of Ophir, which was a region known for its abundance of gold. The seven thousand talents of refined silver was to be used to overlay the walls of the houses, which was a common practice in ancient times to make the walls more beautiful and durable.

5 The gold for things of gold, and the silver for things of silver, and for all manner of work to be made by the hands of artificers. And who then is willing to consecrate his service this day unto the LORD?

This verse is referring to the offering of gold and silver to the Lord. It is asking who is willing to give of their resources to the Lord and dedicate their service to Him. It is a call to action for those who are willing to give of their time and resources to the Lord and His work.

This verse is referring to the leaders of the tribes of Israel who were offering their resources and wealth to help build the temple of the Lord. 7 And gave for the service of the house of God of gold five thousand talents and ten thousand drams, and of silver ten thousand talents, and of brass eighteen thousand talents, and one hundred thousand talents of iron.

This verse is describing the generous donations given to the service of the house of God. Specifically, it states that five thousand talents of gold, ten thousand talents of silver, eighteen thousand talents of brass, and one hundred thousand talents of iron were given. This was a significant amount of wealth, and it demonstrates the generosity of those who donated to the service of the house of God.

8 And they with whom precious stones were found gave them to the treasure of the house of the LORD, by the hand of Jehiel the Gershonite.

This verse is referring to the people of Israel who had gathered to make offerings to the Lord. This verse is referring to the people of Israel who had offered willingly to the Lord. They had given generously and with a perfect heart, and their offering was met with great joy from both the people and King David. The people rejoiced because they had given willingly and with a perfect heart, and King David rejoiced because of the people's generosity.

In this verse, King David is praising God for His goodness and faithfulness. He is thanking God for His love and protection of the people of Israel, and for His everlasting presence in their lives. He is blessing God for His faithfulness and for His everlasting love.

11 Thine, O LORD, is the greatness, and the power, and the glory, and the victory, and the majesty: for all that is in the heaven and in the earth is thine; thine is the kingdom, O LORD, and thou art exalted as head above all.

This verse is a declaration of praise to God for His greatness, power, glory, victory, and majesty. It acknowledges that all that exists in heaven and on earth belongs to God, and that He is the ruler of all. It also acknowledges that God is exalted above all, and is the head of all things.

This verse is emphasizing the power and might of God. It is saying that God is the source of all riches and honor, and that He reigns over all. Furthermore, it states that God has the power and might to make great and to give strength to all. This verse is expressing humility and gratitude to God. It acknowledges that all things come from God and that the people are merely offering what God has already given them. It is a reminder that all of our blessings come from God and that we should be thankful for them.

This verse is expressing gratitude to God for providing the resources to build a temple in His name. The people recognize that all the materials they have gathered to build the temple have come from God and are His alone. They are thankful for His provision and recognize that the temple is ultimately His.

This verse is from the Bible and is part of a prayer offered by King David.
